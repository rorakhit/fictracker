FicTracker

Privacy Policy

Last updated: April 10, 2026

What FicTracker is

FicTracker is a personal reading tracker for Archive of Our Own (AO3) users. It helps you organize your fanfiction library, track reading progress, and discover new works. FicTracker is built and operated by an independent developer.

Data we collect

When you create an account, we store:

  • Your email address
  • Your AO3 username, if you provide one

When you use FicTracker to track your reading, we store:

  • Work metadata imported from AO3 (titles, authors, fandoms, tags, word counts, chapter counts, and other publicly visible stats)
  • Your personal reading data: statuses, star ratings, notes, chapter progress, and reading activity logs
  • Bookshelf names and the works you assign to them

If you subscribe to FicTracker Plus, we store your Stripe customer ID and subscription status. Payment processing is handled entirely by Stripe — we never see or store your credit card number or bank details.

How we use your data

Your data is used solely to operate FicTracker. Specifically:

  • To display your library, reading progress, and stats
  • To generate personalized reading recommendations based on your ratings
  • To check for new chapters on works you are tracking
  • To manage your subscription status

We do not use your data for advertising, profiling, or any purpose beyond providing the FicTracker service.

AI Picks feature (Plus only)

When you use the AI Picks feature, a taste profile is constructed from the metadata of your highly-rated works (titles, authors, fandoms, and relationships/ships). This profile is sent to Anthropic's API (the service that powers Claude) to generate personalized search strategies. No personally identifiable information — such as your email address or AO3 username — is included in this request. Anthropic's data handling is governed by Anthropic's Privacy Policy.

Data sharing and third parties

We do not sell or share your personal data with advertisers or unaffiliated third parties. FicTracker uses the following third-party services to operate:

  • Supabase — database and authentication hosting. Your reading data is stored here with row-level security so no other user can access it.
  • Vercel — web application hosting.
  • Stripe — payment processing for Plus subscriptions. Stripe receives only the information necessary to process your payment.
  • Anthropic — AI service used for the AI Picks feature (Plus only), as described above.

Each of these services has its own privacy policy and data handling practices.

What we don't collect

We do not collect your AO3 password or login credentials. We do not track your browsing activity outside of AO3 pages. We do not use analytics or tracking scripts. We do not store the full text of any fanwork.

How the Chrome extension works

The FicTracker browser extension runs only on archiveofourown.org pages. It reads publicly visible metadata (title, author, tags, chapter count) from AO3 work pages to let you add fics to your library and track chapter progress. The extension communicates only with FicTracker's backend (hosted on Supabase) to sync your reading data. It does not access any other websites or transmit data anywhere else.

How bookmarklets work

FicTracker's bookmarklets run in your browser on AO3 pages when you activate them. They read AO3 page content (bookmark lists, reading history, or individual work pages) and send the extracted metadata to FicTracker's backend. The reading history bookmarklet accesses your private AO3 reading history page, but only the extracted work metadata is transmitted — we never see your AO3 session or cookies.

Data storage and security

Your data is stored in a Supabase-hosted PostgreSQL database in the United States with row-level security enabled. Each user can only access their own reading data. Data is encrypted in transit (TLS) and at rest. The application frontend is hosted on Vercel.

Data retention and deletion

Your data is retained for as long as your account is active. You can remove individual works from your library at any time. If you want your entire account and all associated data permanently deleted, email ro.rakhit@gmail.com and it will be done promptly.

Changes to this policy

If this policy changes in any meaningful way, the update date at the top of this page will be revised. FicTracker is a small, independent project — the goal is always to collect as little data as possible and keep your data private.
